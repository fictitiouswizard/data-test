DD(Justice Fruit) 3 days ago

I realize it’s a bit early but the quarantine boredom is real. I’ve been reflecting on my thoughts and fears around starting projects and I’ve come to some ideas about what that might stem from. I think the big issue I have is that my ideas are often way grander than my skills, so when I sit down to try to do something I’m filled with self doubt. “Is this idea unique enough?” “What if nobody likes it?” “What if I can’t do it?” are all questions I end up asking myself. I think what I’m realizing here is that whatever I end up making needs to be for myself, and simple enough that I can complete it.

Of course, given I’m still kind of a novice I have no idea how easy or hard something is to make. However, I think the biggest thing I have to accept and swallow my pride on is polish. Whatever I make, it will not look like a published triple-A game. It’s going to look messy, buggy, etc. but I’m trying to internalize a motto “Practice is Perfect”. That is to say, what it looks like at the end isn’t really the point. The point is that I have done something and learned something along the way. Just as engineering is an iterative design, my own self-progress must be iterative.

Anyway, as far as actual game progress, I’ve been bouncing around ideas and I think I’ve come up with something that should be easy enough? I will link the game design doc (which, by the way, is harder to make than it seems. I don’t know how to make one, but again that’s ok, the point of it is to help me, not impress others) in the description for anyone curious. My “dream” is to make a sort of army-building game by raising your fallen foes to fight for you. I think what I must accept is it will probably be some dude with zero animations wandering around shooting similarly non-animated projectiles. Maybe we’ll get the zombie army part working. Maybe not. But anything is progress. Anything is something to be proud of.
